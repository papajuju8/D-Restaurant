Desserts

For some of us with a sweet tooth, a meal is incomplete if it does not end with a dessert. The word "dessert" comes from the Old French word "desservir" which means to "clean the table". Tradition says the name comes from entertaining hosts and servants using sweet finishing dishes to denote the end of a meal and a time for people to leave the table. The rush of sugar offers a quick fix to the typical lethargy that comes following a heavy meal, enticing people to get up and leave clean up to the servants.
